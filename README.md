## Applying Roles
During this project, I learned how each role in a Scrum-Agile team helps the project succeed. The product owner focused on what the customer needed and made sure the team was building the right features. The scrum master helped keep the team organized and made sure we stayed on track. The developers worked on building and testing the product. When everyone did their role and communicated clearly, the team worked better and finished tasks more efficiently.

## Completing User Stories
User stories helped break the project into smaller, easier steps. Instead of trying to do everything at once, the team worked on one goal at a time. This made the work less stressful and easier to understand. Feedback during sprint reviews helped improve the product before moving forward.

## Handling Interruptions
There were times when changes happened or things did not go as planned. Agile helped the team adjust quickly without starting over. Instead of getting stuck, the team shifted focus and continued working. This made the process more flexible than the waterfall approach.

## Communication
Communication was very important during this project. When I explained my ideas clearly and responded to others, it helped avoid confusion. I also learned how important it is to listen and think before responding. Clear communication helped the team stay organized and work together better.

## Organizational Tools
Tools like Jira helped keep everything organized. The team could see what tasks were completed, what was in progress, and what still needed to be done. This made it easier to track progress and stay on schedule.

## Evaluating Agile Process
I believe Agile worked very well for this project. It allowed the team to be flexible, get feedback, and improve over time. Compared to waterfall, Agile made it easier to handle changes and stay focused on the goal. While Agile requires strong communication, it creates better teamwork and better results.

## Conclusion
This experience helped me understand how teamwork, communication, and flexibility are important in software development. I feel more confident now and will continue improving my communication and organization skills in future projects.
